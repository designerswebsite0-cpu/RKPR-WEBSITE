EMERGENCY AND SAFETY INFORMATION
Guest-safe guidance
Effective date: 1 January 2026
Last updated: 16 July 2026
Source departments: Security, Engineering, Front Office and Resort Management
1. Purpose
This document provides clear, guest-safe guidance for emergencies and common safety situations at RKPR Resort. It
does not include confidential security procedures, access-control details, surveillance arrangements, staff-only codes,
response deployment plans or other sensitive internal information.
2. Emergency Contact Process
For any emergency, call the Front Office by dialling 100 from the room telephone.
Guests using a mobile phone may call the RKPR Resort emergency assistance line: +91 90080 24000.
State clearly:
Your name
Room or villa number
Exact location
Nature of the emergency
Number of people involved
Whether anyone is injured or unable to move
Remain on the line unless instructed otherwise.
Follow directions from resort staff, emergency responders and public authorities.
Do not return to a restricted area until staff confirm it is safe.
Public emergency numbers in India
112: National emergency response
101: Fire service
102 / 108: Ambulance service, depending on local availability
100: Police service in areas where the legacy number remains active
When possible, contact the resort first so staff can guide responders to the correct location.
3. Fire and Smoke Safety
On arrival
Locate the nearest emergency exit and an alternative exit from your room or villa.
Review the evacuation diagram displayed near the entrance door.

--- Page 2 ---
Keep corridors, balconies and exit routes clear.
Do not cover, damage or interfere with smoke detectors, sprinklers, extinguishers or alarm devices.
Indoor rooms and villas are non-smoking. Smoke only in designated outdoor areas.
If you discover fire or smoke
1. Activate the nearest fire alarm if it is safe to do so.
2. Call the Front Office on extension 100 and provide the exact location.
3. Leave the area immediately using the nearest safe exit.
4. Close doors behind you without locking them.
5. Do not attempt to collect luggage or valuables.
6. Do not use lifts.
7. Proceed to the designated assembly area.
If the alarm sounds
Leave immediately through the nearest safe marked exit.
Use stairs, not lifts.
Stay low if smoke is present.
Cover your nose and mouth with a cloth if available.
Test a closed door with the back of your hand before opening it. Do not open a hot door.
Assist children and guests who require help, without placing yourself in danger.
Follow illuminated exit signs and staff instructions.
If you cannot leave the room
Call extension 100 or the emergency line and provide your room number.
Close doors and block visible gaps with damp towels if smoke is entering.
Move to a balcony or window only when it is safe.
Signal your location to responders without attempting to jump or climb down.
4. Assembly Area
The primary guest assembly area is the Open Events Lawn beside the main arrival driveway, identified by green
emergency assembly signs.
A secondary assembly area may be used when the primary area is affected. Staff will direct guests when required.
At the assembly area:
Remain with your room occupants or family group.
Report missing persons to resort staff immediately.
Do not leave the assembly area without informing staff.
Do not re-enter buildings until official clearance is given.
Keep access routes clear for fire, ambulance and emergency vehicles.
5. Evacuation Guidance

--- Page 3 ---
Remain calm and move promptly.
Walk; do not run, push or return against the direction of evacuation.
Use the nearest safe emergency staircase or external exit.
Do not use passenger lifts during a fire alarm or evacuation.
Guests with reduced mobility should move to the nearest safe refuge point or notify staff immediately.
Parents and guardians are responsible for keeping children with them.
If separated from your group, proceed directly to the assembly area.
Never return for personal belongings.
6. Medical Assistance
Basic first-aid assistance is available 24 hours through the Front Office.
A first-aid room is located beside reception.
A doctor, ambulance or transfer to a clinic or hospital can be arranged subject to availability.
External medical consultations, medicines, ambulance services and hospital charges are payable by the guest
unless covered by insurance.
Guests should inform the resort of serious allergies, mobility limitations or medical conditions when assistance may
be required.
Prescription medicines should remain in their original packaging and under the guest’s control.
The resort is not a hospital and cannot provide specialist or continuous medical care.
Call for urgent help when a person has
Difficulty breathing
Severe chest pain
Uncontrolled bleeding
Loss of consciousness
Suspected stroke symptoms
Severe allergic reaction
Major injury or suspected fracture
Seizure lasting more than five minutes
Serious burns
Suspected poisoning
Do not move a seriously injured person unless they are in immediate danger.
7. Swimming Pool Safety
Main infinity pool
Open daily from 7:00 AM to 9:00 PM.
Use of the pool outside operating hours is prohibited.
Proper swimwear is required.
Follow pool-attendant instructions and posted depth markers.
Do not run, push, dive into shallow areas or use glassware around the pool.
Guests should not swim while under the influence of alcohol or sedating medication.
Guests with medical conditions should seek medical advice before swimming.
Pool decks may become slippery; walk carefully.

Children
Children below 12 must remain under continuous adult supervision.
The presence of resort staff does not replace parental or guardian supervision.
Non-swimmers must use suitable flotation support and remain within arm’s reach of an adult.
Infants must wear approved swim diapers.
Weather and maintenance
Pools may close during lightning, heavy rain, storms, poor visibility, water-treatment procedures or maintenance.
Leave the pool immediately when instructed.
Do not enter a closed or restricted pool area.
8. Child-Safety Rules
Parents and guardians remain responsible for children throughout the stay.
Do not leave children unattended in rooms, villas, pools, balconies, gardens, vehicles or public areas.
Balcony doors and windows should remain secured when young children are present.
Children must not play in corridors, staircases, lifts, parking areas, service zones or near water features.
Kids’ Club entry requires registration and an emergency contact number.
Children below four must be accompanied by a parent or guardian in the Kids’ Club.
Only authorised adults may collect a child from supervised activities.
Inform staff immediately if a child is missing. Provide the child’s name, age, clothing and last known location.
Do not ask other guests to search restricted or hazardous areas.
9. Wildlife and Nature Precautions
RKPR Resort is located in a natural mountain environment. Small animals, insects, reptiles, monkeys and other wildlife
may occasionally be seen.
Do not feed, touch, chase, photograph at close range or attempt to rescue wildlife.
Keep room and villa doors closed, especially after dark.
Do not leave food, fruit or waste on balconies or outside rooms.
Use designated pathways and avoid unlit garden areas at night.
Wear suitable footwear during nature walks and outdoor activities.
Children must remain close to an adult in garden and trail areas.
Report snakes, aggressive animals, bee swarms or injured wildlife to staff.
Do not attempt to capture or kill an animal.
Follow guide instructions during treks and nature walks.
Insect protection
Use insect repellent when outdoors, especially during monsoon and evening hours.
Keep insect screens closed where provided.
Seek medical assistance for severe swelling, breathing difficulty or suspected allergic reaction after a bite or sting.
10. Weather Warnings

--- Page 5 ---
Heavy rain and monsoon
Outdoor activities, trails, pools and transfers may be delayed or closed.
Paths, steps and roads may become slippery.
Avoid streams, steep slopes, drains and flooded areas.
Do not cross water-covered roads or barriers.
Lightning and thunderstorms
Leave pools, lawns, viewpoints and open areas immediately.
Move indoors and stay away from isolated trees, metal railings and exposed structures.
Do not resume outdoor activities until staff confirm conditions are safe.
Strong winds
Stay away from large trees, temporary structures, umbrellas and loose outdoor objects.
Secure balcony doors and avoid exposed viewpoints.
Heat and sun
Drink water regularly.
Use sunscreen, hats and suitable clothing.
Avoid strenuous outdoor activity during the hottest hours.
Seek assistance for dizziness, confusion, fainting or signs of heat illness.
Fog and reduced visibility
Use resort-arranged transport where possible.
Walk only on marked paths.
Allow additional travel time and follow driver instructions.
11. Electrical and Equipment Safety
Use only compatible electrical devices and approved adapters.
Do not overload sockets or use damaged cables.
Keep electrical devices away from water.
Report sparks, burning smells, exposed wires or power faults immediately.
Do not attempt to repair resort equipment.
Do not use candles, incense burners, personal cooking appliances, heating coils or open flames in rooms and
villas.
Follow instructions when using gym, games or recreational equipment.
12. Lift Safety
Follow the displayed capacity limit.
Children should use lifts with an adult.
Do not block lift doors or jump inside the lift.
Do not use lifts during a fire alarm or evacuation.

--- Page 6 ---
If a lift stops unexpectedly, use the emergency call button and wait for assistance.
Do not force the doors open or attempt to climb out.
13. Balcony, Staircase and Slip Safety
Do not sit, stand or climb on balcony railings.
Keep furniture away from balcony edges when children are present.
Use handrails on stairs.
Walk carefully on wet tiles, pool decks and garden paths.
Report spills, damaged flooring or poor lighting to staff.
Wear appropriate footwear in outdoor and recreation areas.
14. Food Allergy and Dining Safety
Inform restaurant staff of allergies before ordering.
State the specific allergen and severity clearly.
Although reasonable precautions are taken, kitchens handle common allergens and cannot guarantee a completely
allergen-free environment.
Do not consume food left unrefrigerated for extended periods.
Refrigerate medicines or specialised foods only after confirming safe storage arrangements with staff.
15. Lost Person or Missing Child
Contact extension 100 immediately.
Provide the person’s name, age, description, clothing, photograph if available and last known location.
Remain available by phone and follow staff instructions.
Do not enter restricted, wooded, water or service areas while searching.
Inform staff promptly if the person is found.
16. Suspicious Item or Unsafe Situation
Do not touch, move, open or examine an unattended suspicious object.
Move away and contact the Front Office.
Report threatening behaviour, damaged barriers, smoke, unusual smells, exposed wiring or blocked exits.
Do not confront another person when doing so may create danger.
Follow staff instructions and respect restricted areas.
17. Road, Parking and Transport Safety
Use marked pedestrian paths and crossings.
Children must be supervised in parking and driveway areas.
Seat belts must be worn where fitted.
Do not exceed the registered passenger capacity of a vehicle.
Declare child-seat requirements when booking transport.
Avoid accepting transport from unverified drivers.
During severe weather, transfers may be delayed, rerouted or cancelled for safety.

18. Accessibility During Emergencies
Guests who may require assistance should inform the Front Office after check-in.
Available support may include:
Manual wheelchair assistance
Step-free evacuation routes where available
Staff guidance to safe refuge or assembly areas
Support for hearing, visual or mobility limitations
Assistance arranging medical transport
Guests should keep essential medication, mobility aids and emergency contact details readily accessible.
19. Guest Responsibilities
Guests are expected to:
Follow safety signs and staff instructions
Supervise children and dependent persons
Report hazards promptly
Use facilities only during operating hours
Avoid restricted, maintenance and staff-only areas
Refrain from tampering with safety equipment
Provide accurate medical and emergency information when requesting assistance
Avoid conduct that places themselves or others at risk

22. Common Guest Questions
Q: What number should I call in an emergency?
A: Dial 100 from the room telephone or call +91 90080 24000 from a mobile phone.
Q: Where is the fire assembly area?
A: The primary assembly area is the Open Events Lawn beside the main arrival driveway, marked with green assembly
signs.
Q: Should I use the lift during a fire alarm?
A: No. Use the nearest safe staircase or external exit.
Q: Is medical assistance available at night?
A: Basic first-aid coordination is available 24 hours through the Front Office. A doctor or ambulance can be arranged
subject to availability.
Q: Can children use the pool alone?
A: No. Children below 12 must remain under continuous adult supervision.
Q: What should I do if I see a snake or wild animal?
A: Keep your distance, do not touch or chase it and contact resort staff immediately.
Q: What happens during severe weather?
A: Outdoor facilities and activities may close, and transport may be delayed or rerouted. Follow staff instructions and
remain indoors when advised.
Q: Does this document include all resort security procedures?
A: No. Sensitive internal security procedures are maintained separately and are not guest-visible.
