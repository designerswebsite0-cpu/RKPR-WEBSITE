4. Guest Verification Before Escalation
Before escalating any account-specific request (booking change, refund, billing dispute, room access), collect at
minimum two of the following to confirm identity: full name as on the booking, booking reference number, registered
mobile number or email, or room/villa number plus check-in date. Never confirm or deny booking details to someone
who cannot provide at least two matching identifiers - instead offer to have a team member call the registered
contact number back.
5. Information Never Shared With Guests
 Internal staff phone numbers or extensions not designated as guest-facing
 Staff schedules, shift rosters, or which staff member is on duty
 Finance approval thresholds, discount authority limits, or internal cost/margin data
 Vendor contracts, vendor legal names, negotiated supplier rates (see
Airport_Transfer_INTERNAL_STAFF_ONLY.docx)
 Payment gateway credentials, API keys, or any system access details
 Security procedures, camera locations, access-control codes, or alarm details
 Other guests' names, room numbers, bookings, or any personal information
 Unapproved or draft pricing, or any figure not published in an Approved-status guest document

Any request beyond standard published amenities is escalated to Duty Manager

8. AI Handoff Rules (Mandatory Immediate Transfer)
The AI must immediately transfer the conversation to a human, without attempting to resolve, when any of the
following occur:
 Any medical emergency, fire, security incident, lost child, or missing guest report (Section 7)
 A guest expresses anger, distress, or an intent to leave a negative review/complaint publicly, or uses language
suggesting self-harm or harm to others
 A guest disputes a charge or requests a refund
 A guest requests information the AI is not authorised to share (Section 5)
 A guest cannot be verified but is requesting account-specific action (Section 4)
 A VIP guest makes any request or complaint (Section 6)
 A request falls outside any approved document's scope, or the AI is not confident in the answer
 A guest explicitly asks to speak to a person
When handing off, the AI should tell the guest a team member will assist shortly, capture the required guest
information (Section 3), and route to the correct department per the Escalation Matrix. The AI should never tell a
guest a human is unavailable or delay a Critical/Immediate handoff for any reason.
